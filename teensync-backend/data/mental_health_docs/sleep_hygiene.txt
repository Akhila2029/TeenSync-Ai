TOPIC: Sleep Hygiene for Teens
CATEGORY: sleep, insomnia, tired, exhausted, can't sleep, fatigue, rest

Teenagers need 8–10 hours of sleep per night. Chronic sleep deprivation worsens anxiety, depression, academic performance, and physical health.

WHY TEENS STRUGGLE WITH SLEEP
- Biological shift: Teen circadian rhythms naturally push sleep later (night owl tendency is real)
- Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin (the sleep hormone)
- Academic pressure and late-night studying
- Social media and phone use at night
- Stress and anxiety making it hard to "turn off"

SLEEP HYGIENE FUNDAMENTALS

1. CONSISTENT SLEEP SCHEDULE
Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day — even on weekends.
This is the single most important sleep habit. Irregular sleep confuses your circadian rhythm.

2. SCREEN CURFEW
Put phones, tablets, and computers away 30–60 minutes before bed.
Blue light tells the brain "it's daytime" and delays melatonin production.
Alternative: Read a physical book, journal, or listen to calm music.

3. BEDROOM ENVIRONMENT
- Cool: 65–68°F (18–20°C) is optimal for sleep
- Dark: Use blackout curtains or an eye mask
- Quiet: Use earplugs or white noise if needed
- Bed = sleep only: Avoid doing homework or watching TV in bed (your brain associates the space with wakefulness)

4. PRE-SLEEP WIND-DOWN ROUTINE
45–60 minutes before bed:
- Dim the lights
- Avoid intense conversations or stressful content
- Try: warm shower/bath, light stretching, 4-7-8 breathing, journaling

5. LIMIT CAFFEINE
No caffeine (coffee, energy drinks, some teas, chocolate) after 2 PM.
Caffeine has a half-life of 5–6 hours — an afternoon coffee is still in your system at bedtime.

6. AVOID NAPS OVER 20 MINUTES
If you must nap, limit it to 20 minutes and not after 3 PM. Longer naps reduce night sleep.

7. EXERCISE (BUT NOT TOO LATE)
Regular exercise improves sleep quality. However, vigorous exercise within 2 hours of bedtime can be stimulating.

WHAT TO DO IF YOU CAN'T SLEEP
- Don't lie in bed awake for more than 20 minutes
- Get up, do something calm in dim light (reading, gentle stretching)
- Return to bed only when sleepy
- Try body scan meditation: slowly relax each body part from toes to head
- Don't watch the clock — it increases anxiety about not sleeping

MENTAL HEALTH AND SLEEP
Sleep deprivation directly worsens:
- Anxiety (the amygdala becomes 60% more reactive without adequate sleep)
- Depression (poor sleep and depression are bidirectionally linked)
- Emotional regulation (harder to manage frustration, sadness, anger)
- Academic performance (memory consolidation happens during sleep)

If you consistently struggle to sleep despite good habits, please speak with a doctor or counselor. Sleep disorders are treatable.
