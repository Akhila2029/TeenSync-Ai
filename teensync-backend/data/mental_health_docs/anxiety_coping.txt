TOPIC: Anxiety Coping Techniques
CATEGORY: anxiety, worry, fear, panic, nervousness

Anxiety is a normal human emotion, but when it becomes overwhelming it can interfere with daily life. Here are evidence-based strategies to manage anxiety:

GROUNDING TECHNIQUE – 5-4-3-2-1
When feeling anxious or panicked, use your five senses to reconnect with the present:
- 5 things you can SEE around you
- 4 things you can TOUCH (and notice the texture)
- 3 things you can HEAR right now
- 2 things you can SMELL
- 1 thing you can TASTE
This pulls attention away from anxious thoughts and into the present moment.

COGNITIVE REFRAMING
Anxiety often comes from catastrophic thinking ("What if the worst happens?").
- Ask: "What is the REALISTIC probability this will happen?"
- Ask: "What is the ACTUAL evidence for and against this fear?"
- Ask: "If it did happen, could I cope? What would I do?"
This helps break the cycle of worry.

WORRY TIME
Set aside 15 minutes each day as your "worry time." When anxiety creeps in outside that window, remind yourself: "I will think about this during worry time." This contains worry rather than fighting it.

JOURNALING
Writing down anxious thoughts externalizes them and reduces their intensity. Try:
- Stream-of-consciousness writing for 5 minutes
- Listing worries, then writing one positive counter-thought for each

PROGRESSIVE MUSCLE RELAXATION
Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds, then release:
Start with feet → calves → thighs → stomach → hands → arms → shoulders → face.
This releases the physical tension anxiety creates in the body.

BOX BREATHING (see breathing_exercises.txt for details)
Inhale 4 counts → Hold 4 counts → Exhale 4 counts → Hold 4 counts.

LIFESTYLE SUPPORT
- Limit caffeine and sugar (they amplify anxiety symptoms)
- Regular sleep (1–2 hours consistent sleep/wake time reduces anxiety significantly)
- Exercise 20–30 minutes daily releases endorphins that counteract anxiety hormones
- Avoid avoidance: facing feared situations gradually reduces anxiety over time

WHEN TO SEEK HELP
If anxiety is preventing you from attending school, maintaining friendships, or enjoying activities you used to love, please speak with a school counselor, therapist, or trusted adult. You deserve support.
