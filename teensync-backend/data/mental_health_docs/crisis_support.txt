TOPIC: Crisis Support and Safety
CATEGORY: crisis, suicidal, self-harm, emergency, danger, hopeless, want to die

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please call emergency services (112 in India) immediately.

RECOGNIZING A CRISIS
Warning signs that someone may be in a mental health crisis:
- Talking about wanting to die or to kill themselves
- Looking for ways to kill themselves (searching online, stockpiling pills)
- Talking about being a burden to others
- Expressing feelings of hopelessness or having no reason to live
- Withdrawing from friends, family, activities
- Giving away prized possessions
- Saying goodbye as if they won't see people again
- Extreme mood swings
- Increased substance use

IMMEDIATE STEPS IF YOU ARE IN CRISIS
1. Tell someone right now — a parent, teacher, friend, counselor
2. Call a crisis helpline (see numbers below)
3. Remove access to means (pills, sharp objects) — put them somewhere less accessible
4. Go to a safe place — be around other people
5. Do not be alone — contact someone to stay with you

IMMEDIATE STEPS IF SOMEONE ELSE IS IN CRISIS
1. Stay calm and stay with them
2. Listen without judgment — do not argue or minimize their feelings
3. Ask directly: "Are you thinking about suicide?" (asking does NOT increase risk; it shows you care)
4. Remove access to means if safely possible
5. Call emergency services or take them to a hospital

SAFETY PLANNING
A safety plan is a personal coping plan for moments of crisis:
1. Warning signs I notice when I'm heading into crisis: ___
2. Coping strategies I can use alone (breathing, journaling): ___
3. People and places that provide distraction: ___
4. People I can ask for help: ___ (name + phone)
5. Professionals I can contact: ___ (counselor, therapist, doctor)
6. Steps to make my environment safer: ___

AFTER A CRISIS
- Recovery is possible. Many people who experience suicidal thoughts go on to live fulfilling lives.
- Please connect with a mental health professional as soon as possible.
- Be gentle with yourself during recovery — healing is not linear.
- Tell trusted people in your life what you experienced so they can support you.

YOU ARE NOT ALONE. HELP IS REAL. RECOVERY IS POSSIBLE.
