TOPIC: Breathing Exercises for Mental Wellness
CATEGORY: breathing, calm, relax, anxiety, panic, stress, overwhelm

Breathing exercises work by activating the parasympathetic nervous system — the body's "rest and digest" mode — which counteracts the fight-or-flight stress response. They are free, instant, and always available.

BOX BREATHING (Square Breathing)
Used by Navy SEALs and therapists alike for acute stress and anxiety.
1. Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 counts
2. Hold your breath for 4 counts
3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 counts
4. Hold empty for 4 counts
5. Repeat 4–6 cycles

Benefits: Calms an overactive nervous system in 2–3 minutes. Excellent before exams, presentations, or conflict situations.

4-7-8 BREATHING
Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil. Particularly effective for sleep and severe anxiety.
1. Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 counts
2. Hold your breath for 7 counts
3. Exhale completely through your mouth making a whoosh sound for 8 counts
4. Repeat 3–4 cycles

Benefits: The long exhale activates the vagus nerve, rapidly triggering calm. Great for falling asleep.

DIAPHRAGMATIC (BELLY) BREATHING
Most people breathe shallowly into the chest when stressed. Belly breathing re-engages the diaphragm.
1. Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly
2. Inhale slowly so your BELLY hand rises but chest hand barely moves
3. Exhale so your belly falls
4. Aim for 6–8 breaths per minute

Benefits: Reduces heart rate, lowers blood pressure, improves oxygen exchange. Can be done anywhere.

ALTERNATE NOSTRIL BREATHING (Nadi Shodhana)
A yogic technique for balance and calm.
1. Close your right nostril with your right thumb
2. Inhale through your left nostril
3. Close your left nostril with your ring finger, release thumb
4. Exhale through your right nostril
5. Inhale through your right nostril
6. Close right nostril, exhale through left
7. Repeat 5–10 cycles

Benefits: Balances left and right brain hemispheres, reduces anxiety, improves focus.

RESONANCE BREATHING (Coherence Breathing)
Breathe at 5 breaths per minute (inhale 6 seconds, exhale 6 seconds).
This syncs your heart rate variability (HRV) and is proven to reduce anxiety and depression symptoms.

EXTENDED EXHALE BREATHING
Simply make your exhale twice as long as your inhale:
- Inhale 3 counts, exhale 6 counts
- Inhale 4 counts, exhale 8 counts
The extended exhale is the key signal to your nervous system to calm down.

QUICK TIPS
- Even 3–5 deep breaths before a stressful situation makes a meaningful difference
- Breathe through your nose when possible (filters air, produces nitric oxide)
- If you feel dizzy, return to normal breathing — you may be hyperventilating (go slower)
- Practice these daily so they are available to you in moments of crisis
