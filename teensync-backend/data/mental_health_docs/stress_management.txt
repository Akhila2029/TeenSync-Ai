TOPIC: Stress Management Tips
CATEGORY: stress, overwhelm, pressure, burnout, deadlines, exhaustion, too much

Stress is your body's natural response to demands placed on it. Short-term stress can motivate, but chronic stress harms mental and physical health. Here's how to manage it effectively.

IDENTIFYING YOUR STRESSORS
Write down: What specifically is stressing me right now?
- Academic pressure (exams, grades, assignments)
- Social stress (friendships, peer pressure, conflict)
- Family stress (expectations, conflict at home)
- Future anxiety (college, career)
Naming the stressor is the first step to managing it.

THE PRIORITY MATRIX (Eisenhower Matrix)
Divide tasks into 4 boxes:
- Urgent + Important → Do immediately
- Not Urgent + Important → Schedule it
- Urgent + Not Important → Delegate or minimize
- Not Urgent + Not Important → Eliminate or do last
This cuts through overwhelm and shows you what truly needs attention now.

TIME BLOCKING
Instead of a long, scary to-do list, assign tasks to time blocks:
- Study block: 9–10 AM
- Break: 10–10:15 AM
- Study block: 10:15–11 AM
Breaks are not optional — they are what make the work blocks sustainable.

THE 2-MINUTE RULE
If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. This clears mental clutter.

PHYSICAL RELEASE OF STRESS
Stress creates cortisol and adrenaline in the body. Physical activity metabolizes them:
- 20-minute walk, run, or bicycle ride
- Dancing to a song you like
- Stretching or yoga
- Even deep sighing (sigh loudly 3 times) helps release tension

SETTING HEALTHY LIMITS
Learn to say no to commitments that exceed your capacity. One phrase: "I really appreciate the invite but I'm at my limit right now."

RECOVERY AND REST
Chronic stress requires deliberate recovery:
- 8–9 hours of sleep for teenagers (non-negotiable for brain recovery)
- Screen-free time before bed (blue light disrupts melatonin)
- One full day per week with reduced demands when possible

TALKING IT OUT
Sharing your stress with a trusted friend, parent, or counselor reduces its weight. You don't need them to solve it — just being heard is healing.

MINDFULNESS FOR STRESS
The STOP technique:
- Stop what you're doing
- Take a deep breath
- Observe what you're feeling in your body and mind (without judgment)
- Proceed with more awareness
This takes 30 seconds and can interrupt the stress spiral.
