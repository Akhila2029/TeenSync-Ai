TOPIC: Depression Support Strategies
CATEGORY: depression, sadness, hopelessness, emptiness, loneliness, low mood

Depression makes everything feel harder and less meaningful. It is not a character flaw or weakness — it is a real condition that responds to treatment and support.

UNDERSTANDING DEPRESSION
Common signs include:
- Persistent low or empty mood lasting more than two weeks
- Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
- Changes in sleep (sleeping too much or too little)
- Low energy, fatigue, difficulty concentrating
- Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
- Withdrawing from friends and family

BEHAVIORAL ACTIVATION (evidence-based approach)
Depression tells you "nothing will feel good, so don't bother." Behavioral activation gently pushes back:
- Make a small list of activities that used to bring you even slight joy
- Schedule ONE activity per day, even for 5–10 minutes
- Notice how you feel BEFORE and AFTER (often slightly better, which builds momentum)
Examples: a short walk, cooking a simple meal, calling a friend, drawing, listening to one song.

SELF-CARE SCAFFOLDING
When depressed, basic self-care becomes difficult. Build scaffolding:
- Sleep: Keep a consistent wake time even if you feel tired
- Nutrition: Eat at least one warm meal per day, even if small
- Movement: A 10-minute walk can lift mood temporarily
- Sunlight: Morning light exposure regulates mood hormones

SOCIAL CONNECTION
Depression often leads to isolation, which deepens it. Even small connections help:
- Send one text to someone you care about
- Spend time around people even if you don't feel like talking
- Vulnerability with a trusted person can ease the weight

CHALLENGING NEGATIVE THOUGHTS
Depression distorts thinking into all-or-nothing, catastrophizing, and self-blame:
- "I'm worthless" → "I'm struggling right now, but that doesn't define my worth"
- "Nothing will ever get better" → "I've felt better before, feelings do change"
- "I'm a burden" → "People who care about me want to support me"

REACHING OUT FOR HELP
Depression is highly treatable with therapy (especially CBT), and sometimes medication.
Please talk to a school counselor, parent, or doctor if you have been feeling this way for more than two weeks.
You do not have to go through this alone.
